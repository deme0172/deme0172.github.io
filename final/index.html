Tokyo: a Tourist's Guide

Top Touristic and Historical Areas of Tokyo

Tokyo is the capital of Japan and one of the world's beautiful metropolises. Here are some of its most popular tourist attractions and historical areas.

Asukuza

Asakusa is one of Tokyo’s many districts and is located in the center of the metropolis’ “low city”, a historical area where an atmosphere of the Tokyo of old survives. During the Edo Period of Japan, from 1603-1867, Asakusa had many Kabuki theaters and a large red-light district. Modern forms of entertainment, such as movie theaters, would also appear in the 1800s and early 1900s. Even though large areas of the district were destroyed during World War 2, it has been rebuilt and been slowly regaining its popularity.

The Tokyo National Museum

The Tokyo National Museum was originally established in 1972 and is the oldest and largest of Japan’s museums. It features six separate buildings, which all specialize in different types of art and exhibitions and one of the largest collections of art and archeological artifacts in Japan. The museum also offers a Japanese-style garden and teahouses, as well as many shops and cafes that are spread out around the grounds.

Sensoji Temple

Sensoji is a Buddhist temple and one of Tokyo’s most colorful and popular temples. It was completed in 645, which makes it Tokyo’s oldest temple. Many events are held throughout the year, the biggest of which is the Sanja Matsuri in May, the temple’s annual festival.

The Meiji Shrine

The Meiji Shrine is dedicated to the deified spirits of Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken and is one of Japan’s most popular shrines. The shrine is a part of a large forested area in Tokyo and offers many walking paths which are excellent for a good stroll. During the New Year, the shrine welcomes over 3 million visitors, even more than any other shrine or temple in the country.

Tokyo Skytree

The Tokyo Skytree is a television broadcasting tower, stands at a height of 634 meters, and stands as the landmark of Tokyo. It is the tallest structure in Japan and it’s one of the tallest buildings in the world. The tower has two observation decks, as well as a large shopping complex and aquarium at the base of the tower.

The Kabuki-Za Theater

The Kabuki-za Theater is one of the most popular theaters in Tokyo. It specializes in the traditional Kabuki drama form, which are highly skilled theatrical performances that are popular with tourists and Japanese locals alike. The theater itself can hold up to 2500 guests, while still keeping an intimate and familiar atmosphere.

Ginza District

Ginza district is Tokyo’s busiest shopping area and it’s been Japan’s commercial center for centuries. It’s lined by various shops and stores, which is pleasant is wander through or to relax in the many tea and coffee shops. All the stores are open on weekends and there is no traffic in the district, which makes it a shopper’s paradise and one of largest pedestrian areas of the world.

Akiharabara

The Akihabara district is famous for its many electronics shops and it has recently gained recognition as the center of Japan’s otaku culture. The district offers all sorts of electronics and otaku merchandise in its stores and shops.
